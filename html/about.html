About

Portuguese soprano Catarina Mouro was born in Lisbon in 1996.

She iniciated her musical studies in 2001 in Academia Musical dos Amigos das Crianças in the violin class. Two years later she took part of the Camera Choir led by the Tenor and professor Vítor Paiva, there she began to grow her love for lyrical singin.

She was part of the cast of various children’s operas such as Pollicino, Let’s Make na Opera and Jeremias Fisher, having performed at a very young age in some of the most important concert houses in the country like Fundação Calouste Gulbenkian, CCB and Culturgest.

In 2011 she was admited in the Escola de Música do Conservatório Nacional (EMCN), in the vocal course, having finished in 2015 with a Chanson and Lied recital that gave her the grade of 18 out of 20.

She attained several awards such as the 1 place in the Concurso Jovem .com in the second category of Chamber Music with the group BelTrio, and simultaneously was classified in 2nd place ex aequo in the category of Advanced Singing in the Prémio Bomtempo, 2014. She also obtained the 2nd place in the second category of singing level II in the CIMCA 2018 with arias such as “Caro Nome”, “Je Veux Vivre” and “Quel Guardo il Cavaliere”.

Her first role was Monica in Menotti’s The Medium in an EMCN opera atelier in 2014.

In 2015 she perform 170 times at Casino Estoril arias such as “Der Hölle Rache” and “Libiamo” in the show, A Noite das Mil Estrelas, from Filipe La Féria; having returned in 2016 for another Filipe La Féria production, O Musical da Minha Vida, singing “Glitter and Be Gay” and “How Can I Go On” 75 times. These shows have helped her achieve a high theatrical performance and a varied range of dance styles such as tap dance and jazz.

In 2016 she participated as Barbarina in Mozart’s Le Nozze di Figaro with Metropolitana Opera Atelier in Centro Cultural de Belém, Teatro Thalia and Fórum Municipal Luísa Todi.

Recently, she participated in Die Zauberflöte at Berlin Opera Academy under direction of Tom Seligman and staging from Katrin Hilbe. There she did Erste Knabe and cover role as Königin der Nacht. Her voice teacher is maestro and pianist Dale Fundling since 2017.

She had masterclasses with David Wakeham, Dominic Barberi, Lucia Mazzaria, Pierre Mak, Ricardo Tamura and Capucine Chiaudani and coach lessons with Nathaniel LaNasa.

She hopes to continue to bring joy and excitement through her voice to the opera lovers.
